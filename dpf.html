Davis Pathfinder

Wayfinding, Visual Identity

The Davis Pathfinder project is a signage system created for the City of Davis, a bike friendly community. Currently, Davis is missing a signage system to guide bikers and pedestrians to key points of interests around the city. In the system I created with my partner, Linda Tran, we divided Davis into 6 districts, each had its own logo and color and created a design for 7 different sign types. Our project was exhibited in the “Design Undergraduate Senior Showcase 2017” hosted by the Design Museum at UC Davis. The image on the left is the poster exhibited at the showcase, click on the image to see the full poster.

These are the 8 logos and colors we chose for each district of Davis. Each features a famous landmark of each district.

For our signs, we focused on a section of North Davis. To understand how these signs will look and function in the space, In Situ photos were rendered. Afterwards, we created full scale black and white prototypes of our signs and placed them in their locations to test the signs. After completing this project in class, I joined Professor Tim McNiel’s group study to assist with the development of the final signage system for the city.
